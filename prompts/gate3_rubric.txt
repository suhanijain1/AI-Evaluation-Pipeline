TASK:
Evaluate the document against each criterion in the rubric. Look for:
1. **Internal contradictions** - Claims that conflict with each other
2. **Missing prerequisites** - Goals stated without addressing blockers
3. **Unrealistic assumptions** - Targets that don't account for constraints
4. **Resource conflicts** - Multiple initiatives competing for same resources
5. **Timeline feasibility** - Deliverables vs stated capacity/constraints

EVALUATION FRAMEWORK:

For each criterion, assess:
- Do the claims logically support each other?
- Are stated goals achievable given the constraints mentioned?
- Are there gaps between ambition and execution capacity?
- Does the document acknowledge and address potential blockers?

EXAMPLES OF STRATEGIC ISSUES:

**Growth vs Constraints:**
- Aggressive growth targets without addressing high attrition/churn
- Expansion plans without sufficient resources or timeline
- Revenue goals that conflict with stated pricing or market size

**Execution Feasibility:**
- Multiple major initiatives competing for same team/budget
- Timeline commitments without capacity analysis
- Technology migrations during critical business periods

**Cost-Benefit Misalignment:**
- ROI claims that don't account for implementation costs
- Savings projections without realistic adoption timelines
- Benefits that require prerequisites not mentioned

SCORING GUIDELINES:
- 90-100: Exceptional - Claims well-supported, feasible, coherent
- 70-89: Strong - Minor gaps or optimistic assumptions, but sound overall
- 50-69: Questionable - Significant feasibility concerns or contradictions
- 30-49: Weak - Major conflicts between goals and constraints
- 0-29: Critical - Fundamentally unrealistic or contradictory

CONSTRAINTS:
- Score range: 0-100 per criterion
- Severity: HIGH (blocks success) | MEDIUM (creates risk) | LOW (minor concern)
- Max 3 findings per criterion
- Be specific with evidence from document
- Focus on logic and feasibility, not stylistic preferences
